iPhone 18 Pro and iPhone 18 Pro Max Prices: Here's What Rumors Say

By: ProTech Team
February 10, 2026
4 min read

In a move that could delight Apple fans worldwide, new leaks suggest that the tech giant is planning to keep the prices of the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro and iPhone 18 Pro Max at the same levels as the current iPhone 17 Pro models. According to a fresh report from Jeff Pu, an equity analyst with investment firm GF Securities, Apple is heavily focused on "cost management" in an effort to prevent price increases for consumers despite expected improvements in specifications and performance.

Apple's Cost Management Strategy

In a research note obtained by MacRumors, analyst Jeff Pu revealed that his firm's recent "supply chain research" indicates Apple is working diligently to keep the starting prices of the iPhone 18 Pro models either "unchanged" or at a "similar level" compared to the iPhone 17 Pro models. This means U.S. consumers can expect the iPhone 18 Pro to start at $1,099 and the iPhone 18 Pro Max to begin at $1,199 – exactly the same as current pricing.

This strategy comes at a time when the tech industry is experiencing significant increases in component costs, particularly for DRAM memory and NAND storage chips. Apple's commitment to maintaining stable pricing makes this achievement even more impressive, as it reflects the company's ability to negotiate with suppliers and find innovative solutions to reduce costs without sacrificing quality.

Important Note

Apple's commitment to keeping prices flat doesn't necessarily mean there won't be significant improvements in the new devices. On the contrary, the iPhone 18 Pro series is expected to deliver substantial upgrades in performance, cameras, and displays.

Negotiations with Key Suppliers

One of the most intriguing aspects of Apple's strategy involves ongoing negotiations with major memory suppliers. According to the report, Apple is engaged in intensive talks with memory industry giants Samsung and SK Hynix to secure relatively "favorable" deals for memory chips. These negotiations come at a critical time, as DRAM and NAND prices are experiencing record highs due to increased demand for high-performance memory across various tech industries, including artificial intelligence and data centers.

But Apple isn't stopping at memory alone. Analyst Pu expects the company to find ways to lower the costs of other core iPhone components as well, including:

Components Targeted for Cost Reduction

  • Display panels: Apple is likely renegotiating prices for OLED panels with major display suppliers to achieve cost savings without compromising the distinctive display quality.
  • Camera systems: Despite expected improvements in cameras, Apple is seeking to enhance manufacturing efficiency and reduce costs through improved production processes and supply chains.
  • Processors and chips: With its own Apple Silicon development, Apple enjoys more control over production costs compared to relying on external suppliers.
  • Batteries and other components: Improvements in battery design and power efficiency could also contribute to reducing overall costs.

Other Analysts Weigh In

Jeff Pu isn't the only analyst predicting this direction from Apple. Renowned analyst Ming-Chi Kuo, known for his accurate predictions about Apple products, has confirmed that the company's plan for the iPhone 18 Pro models includes "avoiding raising prices as much as possible." Kuo predicted that Apple would at least keep starting prices "flat" compared to the iPhone 17 Pro models, reinforcing the expectations outlined in Pu's recent report.

"In the face of global economic challenges and rising component costs, Apple's ability to maintain stable pricing while delivering significant technical improvements reflects its strength in supply chain management and negotiating power with suppliers."

What to Expect from iPhone 18 Pro

Despite the focus on cost management, this doesn't mean Apple will deliver modest devices. Quite the contrary – previous leaks suggest the iPhone 18 Pro series will include notable improvements in several key areas, including a significantly enhanced camera system, displays with more advanced technologies, the A18 Pro processor with superior performance, and possibly increased battery capacity. All these improvements would come – if predictions prove accurate – without a price increase, making the value proposition for consumers much better compared to the previous generation.

Apple is expected to officially unveil the iPhone 18 Pro and iPhone 18 Pro Max at its annual event in September 2026. Until then, we can expect more leaks and rumors to gradually reveal additional details about these highly anticipated devices.

Conclusion

Ultimately, Apple's strategy to keep iPhone 18 Pro prices stable demonstrates the company's commitment to delivering real value to its customers despite economic and industrial challenges. If Apple succeeds in executing this plan, we may witness one of the best value-for-price upgrades in iPhone Pro series history. The balance between technical innovation and reasonable pricing could be the key to Apple's continued success in the highly competitive smartphone market. We'll continue to follow the latest developments and leaks about these exciting devices, and we promise to keep you updated with everything new.
